Nobel Prizesare normally awarded for some of the most important discoveries in various scientific disciplines. Currently, they are awarded for outstanding contributions inPhysics,Chemistry,Physiology or Medicine,Literature,Peace, andEconomic Sciences. This comic suggests that there's an additional award fornotpublishing a mistaken discovery that arose from poorly calibrated measurement equipment. This kind of error may be exemplified by the'faster-than-light' OPERA neutrinos, or just a more general reference to thereplication crisiswhere a lot of results turned out to be false, because people were publishing papers without doing proper statistical analysis. The scientists being awarded fornotreporting the existence of "microbes on Titan" may also be a parody of the (so far unresolved)'possibility of life' on Venus.

The research that produces a discovery worthy of a Nobel Prize is not normally just a single experiment, but a body of work with results that have been replicated, and that has had important further uses. No one is likely to receive an actual prize for spurious results. There have been mistaken research publications that have had significant impact on the wider world, such as the study published inThe Lancetin 1998 that claimed that theMMR vaccinecausedautism; despite being exposed as afraud, this has spurred anti-vaccine sentiment for many years. Another example might be theFleischmann–Ponsclaims of room-temperature fusion.

The title text implies that last year the prize was awarded in error, meaning that the prize committee themselves were, ironically, embarrassed by giving incorrect information. "Double-checking the envelope" is probably a reference to theBest Picture announcement error at the 89th Academy Awards(Oscars). Whether Dr. Jones was awarded the prize in error (requiring an embarrassing climb-down, as soon as the mistake was detected) orshouldhave been the recipient (losing out to some other incorrectly announced winner) is not made clear, but the resulting hurt pride in their case clearly notably persists even after perhaps a full year; the other involved party, meanwhile seems not to require any particular mention.
